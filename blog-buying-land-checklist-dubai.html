Survey Checklist Before
Buying Land in Dubai

June 2026 • 6 min read • By Fortress Surveying Engineering Services

Buying land in Dubai is a significant investment, and relying solely on the title deed and listing photos is a common mistake. An independent pre-purchase survey can reveal issues that aren't visible on paper — protecting you from costly surprises after the deal closes.

Key Point: A pre-purchase survey typically costs a fraction of one percent of a land purchase price — cheap insurance against boundary disputes, hidden utilities, or unbuildable setbacks discovered after you own the land.

The Pre-Purchase Survey Checklist

  1. Boundary verification — confirm the physical plot boundaries on the ground match the registered title deed coordinates exactly
  2. Encroachment check — verify no neighbouring structures, walls or features cross onto the plot you're buying (or vice versa)
  3. Topographic survey — understand actual ground levels; significant level differences can add major cost to foundation works
  4. Utility mapping — check for buried services that might restrict where you can build or require costly diversion
  5. Setback verification — confirm what buildable area remains once DM setback requirements are applied to the actual measured boundaries
  6. Access & road frontage check — verify legal access matches what's described in the sale documents

Why Title Deed Coordinates Aren't Always Enough

Older plots, especially in established Dubai communities, were sometimes surveyed with less precise methods decades ago. Physical boundary markers can also shift, be removed, or simply not exist anymore. A current ground survey is the only reliable way to confirm what you're actually buying matches the paperwork.

Red Flags an Independent Survey Can Catch

  • Plot area smaller than advertised once boundaries are precisely measured
  • Significant ground level changes requiring expensive earthworks before construction
  • Major utility lines crossing the buildable area
  • Neighbouring walls or structures encroaching onto the plot

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a pre-purchase survey take?

Most residential plots can be surveyed within a day, with results available within 24–48 hours — fast enough to fit within typical Dubai property transaction timelines.

Can I use the survey results to renegotiate the price?

If the survey reveals a meaningful discrepancy from what was advertised — such as a smaller buildable area or significant utility constraints — this is commonly used as leverage in price negotiations or as grounds to walk away from the deal.
