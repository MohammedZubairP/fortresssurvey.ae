Setting Out Survey Dubai:
A Complete Guide for Contractors (2026)

June 2026 • 5 min read • By Fortress Surveying Engineering Services

A setting out survey (also called construction layout or staking out) is how approved design coordinates are transferred from drawings to the actual ground on a construction site. It is one of the most critical and time-sensitive survey services in Dubai — any error at this stage directly affects structural positions, floor levels and plot boundaries.

Key Point: Setting out errors that go uncorrected can result in structures being built in the wrong position — causing costly demolition, boundary disputes and DM non-compliance. Always use a licensed Dubai surveyor.

What is a Setting Out Survey?

Setting out is the process of establishing reference marks on site that show where foundations, columns, walls, roads and other elements must be constructed according to the approved drawings. The surveyor works from approved design CAD files and places physical marks — usually nails, paint marks or steel pins — at specified coordinates and levels.

  • Column and pile positions
  • Foundation centrelines and offsets
  • Finished floor levels (FFL) and structural slab levels
  • Road centrelines, kerb lines and drain inverts
  • Boundary pegs and plot corners

When Do You Need a Setting Out Survey in Dubai?

Setting out is required at multiple stages of a Dubai construction project:

StageSetting Out Required
Piling / FoundationPile positions, pile cap centrelines
SubstructureColumn grids, wall lines, slab levels
SuperstructureFloor-by-floor column and slab positions
MEP / ServicesDrain inverts, duct positions, valve chambers
Landscaping / RoadsKerb lines, pavement levels, plot boundaries

Setting Out Accuracy Standards in Dubai

Dubai Municipality and international construction standards (ISO 4463) require:

  • Positional accuracy: ±5–10mm for structural elements
  • Level accuracy: ±3mm for floor slab levels
  • All setting out referenced to established control points in WGS84 / UTM Zone 40N

What drawings do I need to provide for setting out?

We require the approved architectural or structural drawing files in AutoCAD DWG format with coordinates. If your drawings are in a local grid, we can transform them to WGS84 / UTM Zone 40N before staking.
