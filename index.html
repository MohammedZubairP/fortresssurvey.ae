Our Land Surveying Services in Dubai

1. Topographic Survey Dubai

A topographic survey (also known as a topo survey, land survey or contour survey) is the foundation of any development or construction project in Dubai. Our topographic surveys capture the precise three-dimensional shape and features of your site — including ground levels, contours, spot heights, existing structures, boundaries, trees, utilities and road information. All topographic surveys are delivered in WGS84 coordinates, UTM Zone 40N projection and EGM2008 height datum as required by Dubai Municipality. Deliverables include full AutoCAD DWG drawings and PDF plans formatted to DM title block standards, accepted at first submission.

Fortress Surveying provides topographic surveys across all Dubai zones including Downtown Dubai, Business Bay, Dubai Marina, Palm Jumeirah, DIFC, Jumeirah, JBR, Al Quoz, Dubai Investment Park, Dubai South, Jebel Ali, Deira, Bur Dubai, Mirdif, Dubai Creek, Dubai Hills Estate, Mohammed Bin Rashid City and Al Rashidiya, with 24–72 hour turnaround.

2. Setting Out Survey Dubai (Construction Layout)

Setting out (also called construction layout, stakeout or setting-out survey) is the process of establishing precise reference marks on site that define exactly where each structural element — columns, pile caps, foundations, walls, floor slabs and road alignments — must be constructed in accordance with approved drawings. In Dubai, all setting-out work must reference WGS84 control points and comply with ISO 4463 standards for construction measurement. Fortress Surveying engineers use Leica TS16 and Trimble S7 total stations achieving ±2mm positional accuracy, exceeding DM requirements.

Our setting out services cover piling programs, foundation layouts, column grids, floor-by-floor structural positioning, MEP services, road and infrastructure alignment, landscaping levels and plot boundary demarcation. We mobilise within 24 hours across all Dubai zones and provide a signed setting out field report with as-staked coordinates after every job — a requirement for DM inspection records.

3. As-Built Survey Dubai

An as-built survey is a mandatory requirement for obtaining a Dubai Municipality Completion Certificate and the No Objection Certificate (NOC) required before building handover and registration with Dubai Land Department. An as-built survey documents the actual constructed positions, floor areas, levels and dimensions of a completed structure compared to the original approved permit drawings. Fortress Surveying is a DM-registered licensed surveyor authorised to certify and submit as-built surveys through the DM e-submission portal.

Our as-built survey service includes full field measurement of all completed elements, AutoCAD DWG production to DM title block format, preparation of the variation comparison table, digital submission via the DM portal, and all correspondence with DM reviewers. We maintain a 99% first-submission acceptance rate. We cover villas, apartments, towers, commercial buildings, warehouses, mosques, schools, hotels and infrastructure across Dubai.

4. Aerial Survey and Drone Mapping Dubai

Fortress Surveying holds a Dubai Civil Aviation Authority (DCAA) approved UAV operations permit, allowing us to conduct professional drone survey flights across Dubai for land surveying, mapping and volumetric purposes. Our aerial survey service delivers high-resolution orthomosaic images, digital terrain models (DTM), digital surface models (DSM), point clouds and volumetric calculations using survey-grade UAV platforms and Pix4D/Metashape photogrammetry processing software.

Drone aerial surveys are ideal for large plots, construction progress monitoring, earthwork volume calculations, stockpile measurement, infrastructure corridor mapping, solar site surveys, agricultural land surveys and master plan area mapping in Dubai. We can cover up to 500 hectares per day with ground sample distance (GSD) of 2–3 cm/pixel and horizontal accuracy of ±3–5cm with ground control points. All drone survey data is referenced to WGS84 and delivered with full metadata for GIS integration.

5. 3D Laser Scanning and Scan-to-BIM Dubai

3D terrestrial laser scanning provides millimetre-accurate documentation of existing buildings, structures and infrastructure in Dubai. Using the Leica RTC360 scanner capable of capturing up to 2 million points per second, Fortress Surveying creates dense, accurate point clouds of any structure — from heritage buildings in Al Fahidi to modern high-rises in Dubai Marina. Point clouds are registered and delivered in RCP, LAS, E57 and RCS formats compatible with AutoCAD, Revit, Navisworks and all major AEC software platforms.

Our Scan-to-BIM service converts laser scan point clouds into fully parametric Autodesk Revit models at LOD 200, 300 or 400 for as-built BIM documentation, renovation design, clash detection, facility management and handover packages. We work with developers, architects, contractors and facilities managers across Dubai who require accurate existing-conditions models as a starting point for new design, fit-out or major MEP works.

6. Utility Mapping and GPR Survey Dubai

Before any excavation in Dubai, contractors are legally required to identify and locate all buried utilities to prevent strikes, injury and costly project delays. Fortress Surveying uses ground-penetrating radar (GPR) and radio detection technology to locate all buried utilities including DEWA electricity cables, water mains, gas pipelines, Etisalat and du telecommunications cables, RTA drainage systems and private utility networks. Our utility mapping service covers all ground types including asphalt, concrete, sand and landscaping across Dubai.

Deliverables include georeferenced utility maps overlaid on topographic surveys, with depth estimates and material identification where possible, produced in AutoCAD DWG format. We follow PAS 128 and ASCE 38 utility survey quality levels and can provide data for DEWA, RTA, Etisalat asset records and DM submission packages. We also offer CCTV drain inspection surveys to document condition of existing drainage assets before and after construction.

7. Structural Monitoring and Gauge Platform

Structural monitoring is a critical safety requirement for deep excavation projects, tunnelling operations, projects adjacent to existing structures, heritage buildings and long-term infrastructure assets in Dubai. Fortress Surveying installs automated sensor networks — including settlement markers, tilt sensors, crack gauges, piezometers and total station prism networks — and connects them to our proprietary Gauge monitoring platform for real-time data collection, dashboard visualisation and automated alert generation.

The Gauge platform provides project teams, contractors and consultants with live settlement and deformation graphs, threshold alarm management, SMS and email alerts when movement exceeds pre-set trigger levels, and automated PDF report generation. Gauge is a cloud-based platform accessible 24/7 from any device, with multi-user access and contractor data sharing portals. Our monitoring service includes dilapidation surveys before and after construction to protect adjacent property owners from unfounded claims.

8. GNSS / GPS Control Survey Dubai

Precise geodetic control networks are the foundation of all survey work in Dubai. Fortress Surveying establishes primary and secondary control networks using Leica GS18 and Trimble R12 GNSS receivers in static observation mode (for network-grade accuracy) and RTK mode (for site-level control). All control networks are established in WGS84 coordinates referenced to the UAE National Geodetic Network and conform to DM spatial data requirements. Static GPS campaigns are designed and processed using Leica Infinity and Trimble Business Center software to produce control point reports with full accuracy statistics.

9. Traffic Survey Dubai

Fortress Surveying provides traffic survey services in Dubai for RTA submissions, traffic impact assessments (TIA) and transport studies required as part of development permit applications. Our traffic survey team deploys automatic traffic counters (ATC), video surveys and manual classified counts at key intersections and road sections. Data is processed and reported in the format required by RTA Dubai for all traffic study submissions. We cover all major road networks, intersections, roundabouts and interchange areas across Dubai.

Dubai Municipality Survey Requirements — What You Need to Know

All surveys submitted to Dubai Municipality must comply with the DM Spatial Data Infrastructure requirements. These include: use of WGS84 Geographic Coordinate System (GCS); projection in Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) Zone 40N; heights referenced to EGM2008 global geoid model; AutoCAD DWG file format in the DM-approved template; digital submission via the DM e-submission portal; and certification by a DM-approved licensed surveyor. Fortress Surveying's deliverables meet all these requirements as standard — we do not charge extra for DM-compliant formatting.

The most common reasons for DM survey rejection include: incorrect coordinate system (surveys done in a local grid not transformed to WGS84); wrong height datum; missing or incorrectly formatted title block; survey certified by a non-DM-registered surveyor; incomplete coverage of the site; incorrect floor area calculation method; and missing comparison table in as-built surveys. Fortress Surveying eliminates all these risks through our quality-checked production workflow and 99% first-submission acceptance track record.
