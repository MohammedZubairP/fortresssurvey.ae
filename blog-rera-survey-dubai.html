RERA Survey Requirements
for Developers in Dubai

June 2026 • 5 min read • By Fortress Surveying Engineering Services

RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Agency) oversees off-plan sales, escrow accounts and project registration for Dubai developers. While RERA itself doesn't perform surveys, several stages of the off-plan and handover process depend directly on accurate survey documentation.

Key Point: RERA project registration and unit handover both rely on underlying DLD title surveys and DM as-built documentation — survey accuracy errors can delay escrow release and unit registration.

Where Surveys Fit Into the RERA Process

  • Pre-registration — accurate plot boundary survey confirming the land matches title deed records before project registration
  • Construction milestones — for escrow release schedules tied to physical construction progress verification
  • Unit handover — as-built surveys confirming final unit areas match what was sold, often required for accurate area certification
  • Common area registration — boundary and area surveys for shared facilities, car parks and amenities

Area Certification & Discrepancies

One of the most sensitive issues in off-plan developments is when constructed unit areas differ from sold areas. An independent as-built survey provides developers with defensible, DM-compliant documentation of actual areas — protecting against buyer disputes during handover.

Common Area & Master Community Surveys

Master developments with shared roads, parks and facilities require boundary surveys defining what belongs to individual plot owners versus the master community — directly affecting service charge calculations and RERA-regulated community management disclosures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does RERA require a specific surveyor accreditation?

RERA itself doesn't directly accredit surveyors, but underlying DLD and DM submissions require DM-registered licensed surveyors — which Fortress Surveying maintains for all project work.

Can a survey help resolve a unit area dispute during handover?

Yes — an independent as-built survey with measured floor areas is the standard evidence used to confirm or dispute claimed unit sizes during handover disagreements.
