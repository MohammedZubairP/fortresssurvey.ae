Topographic Survey in Dubai:
DM Requirements, Process & Cost (2026)

June 2026 • 6 min read • By Fortress Surveying Engineering Services

A topographic survey is one of the most frequently requested land survey types in Dubai. Whether you are a developer planning a new project, a contractor preparing construction drawings, or a consultant submitting to Dubai Municipality, understanding what a topographic survey involves — and what DM requires — can save you significant time and money.

Quick Summary: A topographic survey in Dubai maps all natural and man-made features of a site including levels, boundaries, utilities and structures. All surveys must be delivered in WGS84 coordinates, UTM Zone 40N projection and EGM2008 height datum for DM acceptance.

What is a Topographic Survey?

A topographic survey (also called a topo survey or land survey) captures the precise three-dimensional shape and features of a piece of land. This includes:

  • Ground levels and contour lines at specified intervals
  • Spot heights at key locations
  • Existing structures, walls and boundaries
  • Trees, landscaping and paved areas
  • Visible utility covers, chambers and above-ground services
  • Road and kerb levels at site frontages

The result is a detailed CAD drawing that engineers, architects and planners use as the base for all subsequent design work.

Dubai Municipality Requirements for Topographic Surveys

Dubai Municipality (DM) has strict requirements for all survey submissions. Failure to meet these requirements is the most common reason for first-submission rejections. Fortress Surveying delivers all topo surveys compliant with these standards:

The Topographic Survey Process in Dubai

A typical topographic survey with Fortress Surveying follows these stages:

  1. Site reconnaissance and control setup — establishing or referencing existing control points in WGS84
  2. Field survey — total station measurement of all features and levels across the site
  3. Data processing — generating DTM, contours and feature layers in CAD
  4. Quality check — independent review of all data before drawing production
  5. Drawing production — DM-compliant DWG and PDF deliverables
  6. Delivery — typically within 24–72 hours of fieldwork completion

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a topographic survey take in Dubai?

Field survey typically takes half a day to one day depending on site size. Final CAD drawings are delivered within 24–72 hours of completing fieldwork. Fortress Surveying can mobilise within 24 hours of project confirmation.

Do I need a topographic survey for a Dubai Municipality submission?

Yes — a DM-compliant topographic survey is required for building permit applications, plot development applications, infrastructure design submissions and most planning applications across Dubai.

What coordinate system does Dubai Municipality use?

Dubai Municipality requires all survey data in WGS84 coordinates, UTM Zone 40N projection, with heights referenced to the EGM2008 geoid model. All Fortress Surveying deliverables meet this standard.

Can Fortress Surveying submit the survey directly to Dubai Municipality?

Yes. Our licensed engineers are registered with DM and can handle the full e-submission process on your behalf, including the digital submission portal upload and correspondence with DM reviewers.
